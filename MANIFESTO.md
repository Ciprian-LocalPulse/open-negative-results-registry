# The Dark Data Medicine Manifesto

## We publish what failed.

Somewhere, right now, a lab is running an experiment that already failed
somewhere else. Not because anyone was careless. Not because the science was
bad. Because the result was negative, and negative results have nowhere to
go.

They don't get published. They don't get cited. They don't get indexed,
searched, or found. They get filed away — in a lab notebook, a private
database, a researcher's memory — and they die there, quietly, while the
next lab, the next country, the next decade repeats the same experiment
and arrives at the same dead end.

We think that's a waste we can no longer afford.

## What we believe

**A negative result is not a failure. It is data.**
A drug that didn't work, a hypothesis that wasn't confirmed, a trial that
missed its endpoint — these are not embarrassments to bury. They are the
boundaries of what we know, mapped one honest experiment at a time.

**Knowledge that isn't findable might as well not exist.**
It doesn't matter how rigorous an experiment was if the only record of it
sits on one hard drive, in one lab, known to one person. A result that
can't be searched can't save anyone else's time, funding, or risk.

**The people best positioned to share this data are the least equipped to
do it alone.**
Clinicians and researchers are not required to be software engineers. If
contributing a negative result requires learning Git, most of it will
never get contributed. So we built the on-ramp instead of writing the
rulebook.

**Trust is earned through review, not assumed through automation.**
Every entry in this registry passes through a human curator before it
becomes part of the public record — checked for duplication, checked for
privacy, checked for whether it's actually a negative result and not just
an inconvenient one. Automation can suggest. It cannot decide.

**Credit should follow contribution.**
A researcher who shares a negative result is doing real scientific work,
and deserves the same currency as any other contribution: attribution,
citation, a permanent record with their name on it.

**Open means open. Free means free.**
No paywall to search it. No account required to read it. No fee to
contribute. The moment any of that changes, the registry stops solving the
problem it was built for.

## What we reject

We reject the idea that publication bias is just how science works and
always will. It's a structural failure, and structural failures can be
structurally fixed.

We reject the idea that a negative result needs a full peer-reviewed
manuscript to be worth recording. Most of them will never get one. That
shouldn't mean they get nothing.

We reject the idea that "no one will use it" is a reason not to build it.
Someone has to go first. An empty registry with the right foundation is
worth more than no registry at all.

## What we're building

A free, structured, openly licensed record of negative and null results in
medicine — searchable by disease, by intervention, by outcome — so that
the next researcher standing where a hundred others already stood can
find out, in five minutes, what didn't work before they spend five years
finding out the hard way.

Every failed experiment recorded here is a future one that doesn't have to
happen.

**No negative result dies in a drawer again.**
