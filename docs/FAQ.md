# Frequently Asked Questions

## What is the Open Negative Results Registry?

The Open Negative Results Registry is a public documentation and preservation space for negative, null, and unpublished scientific findings, with an initial focus on medicine and the life sciences.

## What counts as a negative result?

A negative result is a finding that does not support the original hypothesis or does not show the expected effect. This includes null results, failed replications, non-significant outcomes, and terminated or inconclusive studies when the final outcome is clearly documented.

## Why are negative results important?

Negative results help reduce publication bias, prevent redundant research, improve meta-analyses, and strengthen scientific integrity. They also help researchers avoid repeating experiments that have already failed elsewhere.

## Who can submit data?

Researchers, clinicians, students, institutions, and independent investigators may submit eligible entries, provided the information is accurate, ethical, and does not violate privacy or legal restrictions.

## Do I need GitHub experience to contribute?

No. The project is designed so that non-technical contributors can still participate through guided submission methods and templates.

## What information should a submission include?

A good submission should include a title, field, hypothesis, method, outcome, reason the result matters, source references if available, and appropriate metadata.

## Are unpublished results allowed?

Yes, if the submission is ethically shareable and does not contain restricted, confidential, or identifiable information that should not be made public.

## Is this repository only for medicine?

The initial scope is medicine and the life sciences, but the structure can be expanded later if the governance model allows it.

## How is the data verified?

Submissions should be reviewed for clarity, relevance, and consistency. Human curation is recommended before inclusion in the public dataset.

## Can this repository be cited?

Yes. The repository should be cited in academic or research contexts according to the citation guidance provided in the project documentation.
